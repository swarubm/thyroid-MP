Other Important Thyroid Tests

Thyroid Antibodies

Tests for autoimmune thyroid conditions like Hashimoto's disease and Graves' disease.

  • TPO Antibodies: Thyroid peroxidase antibodies
  • TG Antibodies: Thyroglobulin antibodies
  • TSI: Thyroid stimulating immunoglobulins
Reverse T3 (rT3)

Inactive form of T3 that can indicate stress, illness, or poor conversion of T4 to T3.

  • Normal Range: 10-24 ng/dL
  • High Levels: May indicate stress or illness
  • Clinical Use: Assess T4 to T3 conversion
Thyroglobulin

Protein produced by the thyroid gland, used as a tumor marker after thyroid cancer treatment.

  • Normal Range: 1.5-30 ng/mL
  • High Levels: May indicate thyroid cancer recurrence
  • Clinical Use: Cancer monitoring
Calcitonin

Hormone produced by C-cells in the thyroid, used to detect medullary thyroid cancer.

  • Normal Range: < 10 pg/mL
  • High Levels: May indicate medullary thyroid cancer
  • Clinical Use: Cancer screening

Testing Guidelines & Recommendations

When to Test:
  • Unexplained fatigue or weight changes
  • Family history of thyroid disease
  • Pregnancy or planning pregnancy
  • Age 60+ (routine screening)
  • Autoimmune conditions
  • Previous thyroid treatment
Testing Frequency:
  • Normal function: Every 2-3 years
  • Subclinical: Every 6-12 months
  • On medication: Every 6-12 months
  • Pregnancy: Each trimester
  • Post-treatment: Every 3-6 months
Important Notes
  • Test in the morning for most accurate results
  • Fasting is not required for most thyroid tests
  • Some medications can affect thyroid test results
  • Pregnancy can alter normal ranges
  • Always consult with your healthcare provider for interpretation
